SSO ID Rajasthan — Single Sign-On for Government Services

SSO ID (Single Sign-On) Rajasthan is the unified digital identity across Rajasthan Government portals. With a single credential you can access dozens of services, submit applications, check status, and interact with departments securely and conveniently.

Create SSO ID

What is SSO ID Rajasthan?

SSO ID Rajasthan is an integrated authentication system that allows citizens, businesses, and government employees to use a single username and password—an SSO credential—to log into multiple government applications. Instead of maintaining separate accounts for each portal, users create one SSO ID that acts as their digital identity within the state ecosystem. The SSO platform handles authentication, account management, and secure data exchange with upstream departmental applications.

Key Benefits

Convenience: One account for many services eliminates repeated registrations and reduces form-filling time. Time-saving: Faster access to services like certificates, permits, and social welfare schemes. Improved user experience: Centralized dashboards show application status across departments. Security: The platform implements modern authentication methods such as OTP verification, multi-factor options and secure password policies. Transparency: Citizens can track application status end-to-end and receive official notifications.

Who can use it and what services are available?

Anyone with a valid mobile number and an Aadhaar or other accepted identity proof can register for an SSO ID. The platform integrates services from revenue, transport, health, education, labor, and municipal departments. Typical services include birth/death certificates, caste/ income certificates, trade licenses, professional registrations, vehicle-related services, and benefit disbursal portals. Many services provide the option to submit documents electronically and receive digitally signed certificates.

How to register for an SSO ID (step-by-step)

  1. Visit the Rajasthan SSO portal (official state SSO gateway).
  2. Click on "Sign Up" or "Create SSO ID" and choose the user type (Citizen/Employee/Business).
  3. Enter your mobile number and verify it using the OTP sent by the portal.
  4. Provide identity details: Aadhaar, PAN, or other government ID as required. Some services may require Aadhaar-based eKYC.
  5. Set a secure password and choose security questions or enable MFA if available.
  6. Complete the profile by adding contact information and optional documents.
  7. Once verified, you will receive a confirmation and can log in immediately to access services.

Logging in and using services

After logging in with your SSO ID, users typically land on a dashboard that displays enrolled services, recent applications, and alerts. To use a departmental application, click the service tile or link and the SSO gateway performs identity and session transfer without asking for credentials again. Most services also allow profile-based autofill to speed up form submission.

Security and privacy

SSO platforms use encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) and server-side safeguards to protect user credentials. For sensitive transactions, additional verification like OTP, biometric or time-based one-time passwords (where enabled) provide extra layers of security. Users should pick strong, unique passwords for their SSO ID, enable multi-factor authentication if available, and avoid sharing OTPs or login details. Review the portal's privacy policy to understand how data is used and shared with departments.

Common issues and troubleshooting

If you don’t receive OTPs, ensure your mobile number is active and not on DND; try resending the OTP after a short wait. For Aadhaar-related eKYC failures, check that your Aadhaar is active and the linking details match. Use the "Forgot Password" flow to reset credentials, and contact SSO helpdesk or the concerned department when stuck. Keep screenshots or reference numbers of submissions when following up.

Tips and best practices

  • Use a personal email and mobile that you control; update them when changed.
  • Enable two-factor authentication if the portal provides it.
  • Keep digital copies of frequently used documents to speed up applications.
  • When sharing documents online, only upload official, scanned copies—not photographs of sensitive documents unless required.
  • Periodically review your account activity and contact support on suspicious actions.

SSO ID Rajasthan is designed to simplify citizen interactions with government. By adopting a single, secure login model it reduces friction, increases transparency, and helps departmental services reach citizens more efficiently. Whether you need certificates, licenses, or to check scheme eligibility, your SSO ID is the gateway to a growing set of digital public services in Rajasthan.
